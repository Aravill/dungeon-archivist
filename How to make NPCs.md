# Tiers
## Tier 1: Retainers
- One-off NPCs
- Appear only a few times to fulfill specific tasks
- Do not have any significant impact on the story
- Only need names if players are to have a social encounter with them (an innkeeper may need a name, a guardsman asking for papers doesn't)
- Example: Hama (The Lord of the Rings)
	- Greets the heroes when they arrive to Edoras, dies a few scenes later
- Have as many of these as you'd like, the players likely won't remember them
### Design Checklist
- [ ] Race
- [ ] Appearance
- [ ] Name

## Tier 2: Reoccurring Characters
- Reusable characters
- Like guest stars in a TV show
- Should aid or hinder the players, or provide them with social encounters
- Players can grow attached to them or start hating them
- Example: Arwen (The Lord of the Rings)
	- Love interest of a hero, occasionally appears to the hero in a dream as a relief from their struggle
- Only a dozen of these should be featured in a campaign segment
### Design Checklist
- [ ] Race
- [ ] Age
- [ ] Appearance
- [ ] Mannerism
- [ ] Backstory outline
	- [ ] Motivation
	- [ ] Occupation
	- [ ] Relationships
- [ ] Name

## Tier 3: Major Figures
- Regularly appearing characters
- Party's primary allies or adversaries
- Do not have to appear constantly, but have major impact on the story
- Example: Theoden (The Lord of the Rings)
	- King of a kingdom the heroes are trying to get aid from, convincing him to help is one a central plot point of several adventures
- Only up to four per campaign segment

# Creating Names
- Easier to make up names for fleshed out characters
- Use placeholders until the end (like "Knight" or "Queen")
- Except when you come up with a really cool name
## Cheat sheet
- Having a name cheat sheet is handy
- Used for ad-hoc created characters
- You can generate names using any online generator
- Make something like this, cross out the names you've already used
- Only 4 per race are necessary, 2 male and 2 female
