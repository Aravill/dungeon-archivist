# A Need for Speed
- 5th edition combat has a tendency of being slow and static
## Roll initiative
- We like hearing "Roll for initiative" it... or do we?
	- Encounters are either shocking or expected
	- Expected encounters have build up
	- Climax - the enemies are finally within reach, we get to fight them
	- The DM says a variation of "Roll for initiative"
	- Now we're rolling initiative, waiting for 5+ minutes (depending on enemy count) to see how much longer we have to wait before doing something
	- It stops the excitement dead in its tracks
- I like to speed it up
- Players only ever roll for themselves, they're done quickly
- Rolling for all the NPCs and recording their initiative takes time
- Solutions?
	- a) Pre-roll the encounter initiative - you already know what NPCs will be present anyway
	- b) Grouped initiative rolls - don't roll for single NPCs, group them up, like "cultists" and "hounds"; this reduces rolls from tens to a couple
- Always keep in mind
	- Less rolls = faster
	- More rolls = dynamic
## Players taking too long / not paying attention
- In my experience happens when the combat is too predictable
- Why pay attention if nothing is happening to the PC, and the PC cannot do anything?
- Make combat less predictable and static by changing conditions
