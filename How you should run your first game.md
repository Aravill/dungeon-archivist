# Pre-made modules
- The internet is full of amazing one-shot games
- Use something pre-made to see how it's structured and paced
- Avoid using campaign modules (like Curse of Strahd)
	- They're great, but super long and complicated
- Try things like
	- [Delian Tomb](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1T_IyzoluvleQmMg0o3M4dckm3QMro_nuVaQ4YuYDZl4/edit?tab=t.0) (MCDM)
	- [Against the cult of the Reptile God](https://www.dmsguild.com/en/product/210806/classic-modules-today-n1-against-the-cult-of-the-reptile-god-5e) (?)
	- [The Wild Sheep Chase](https://winghornpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/the_wild_sheep_chase_v2.pdf)
- Look for modules that
	- Are simple
	- Span no more than 2 sessions
# Homebrew your adventure
- This is much more difficult, especially for someone without experience... but let's be honest, it's also what all of us really want to do
- For this approach, still go with a pre-made adventure
	- Don't run it, just read it
	- See how it's structured, what it keeps track of
		- Story beats, locations, loot, characters
	- This will give you an idea of how your preparation should look
